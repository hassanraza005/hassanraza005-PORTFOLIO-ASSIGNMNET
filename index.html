Abdul Qadeer Khan was a Pakistani scientist. He played a pivotal role in nuclear weapons program development in Pakistan and abroad.
His efforts to develop nuclear weapons through the enrichment of uranium and not plutonium allowed Pakistan to successfully develop weapons,
which it had struggled to do previously.


Biographic


Early Life:
Born on April 1, 1936, in Bhopal, India.

Education:
Completed his early education in India and later moved to Pakistan after its independence in 1947.

Career Beginnings:
Started his career as a metallurgist in Europe before returning to Pakistan in the 1970s.

Nuclear Program:
Played a pivotal role in Pakistan's nuclear weapons program, becoming known as the "father of the Pakistani nuclear bomb."

Controversies:
Involved in the proliferation of nuclear technology, leading to controversies and international sanctions.

Legacy:
Despite controversies, remains a revered figure in Pakistan for his contributions to the country's nuclear program.

Later Years:
Continued to be active in public life, advocating for science education and research until his passing on October 10, 2021, in Islamabad, Pakistan.
